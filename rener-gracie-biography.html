    Who is Rener Gracie?

    Rener Gracie is an American Brazilian jiu-jitsu practitioner and chief instructor by profession.

    Moreover, he is a chief instructor at Gracie University and co-founder of the Gracie University online curriculum.

    Additionally, he is a member of the Gracie Family which is a prominent martial arts family from Rio de Janeiro, Brazil.

    Rener Gracie: Birth Facts, Early Life

    Rener Gracie was born on April 24, 1984, in Torrance, California, United States. Currently, he is 38 years old.

    Rener is the second eldest son of Grandmaster Rorion Gracie and Suzanne Gracie.

    He grew up with brothers, Ryron Gracie, Ralek Gracie, and Reylan, and one younger sister, Segina.

    Likewise, he also has two half-sisters, Rose and Riane Gracie from his father’s first marriage along with three half-brothers, Roran, Renon, and Ricon, from his father’s third marriage.

    Their grandfather is Grand Master Helio Gracie from whom he received his black belt in 2002.

    However, he got his 4th degree Black Belt in Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu under his father, Rorion Gracie.

    Moreover, he is of American Nationality. Her birth sign is Scorpio.

    Speaking of his education, Rener spent his twenty-five years at the Gracie Academy under the education of Rorion and Helio Gracie.

    Rener Gracie: Career, Professional Life

    Rener is better known for his skills as a Gracie jiu-jitsu instructor and for making jiu-jitsu more popular among people using web-based distance learning programs.

    He was already a jiu-jitsu instructor at 13 years old.

    As part of Gracie University, he has contributed a lot to the Gracie Family. Rener participated in the 2003 Pan American Jiu-Jitsu Championship and clinched the 3rd title.

    The same year, he competed in the first Southern California Pro-Am Invitational wearing a judogi.

    He took first place defeating Joe Stevenson (UFC Fighter), Cassio Werneck (BJJ World Champion), Jason “Mayhem” Miller (UFC Fighter), and Tyrone Glover.

    The following year, he retired from a jiu-jitsu competition and focused on the street self-defense aspect of jiu-jitsu.

    Moreover, he is also the founder and CEO of a clothing company, Quikflip Apparel.

    Quick flip was featured on ABC’s Shark Tank and it was listed as one of the “Best Inventions of 2019” by Time Magazine.
